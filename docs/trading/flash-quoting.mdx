# Flash Quoting

Flash Quoting lets market makers submit orders **far exceeding their balance** — typically ~20x. The matching engine's linear solver ensures the MM's balance never goes negative, making this safe without per-order collateral locking.

## The Problem

Traditional prediction markets lock collateral per order:

| Market | Order | Collateral Locked |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| Bitcoin > \$100k | Bid \$500 @ 55% | \$275 |
| ETH > \$5k | Bid \$500 @ 40% | \$200 |
| Election | Bid \$500 @ 60% | \$300 |
| **Total** | | **\$775** |

A normal user with \$100 can place \$100 of bids and \$100 of asks — **\$200 total**. To quote 10 markets, they'd need 10x the capital.

## The Solution

Flash Quote MMs don't lock collateral per order. Instead, the matching engine adds a single constraint to its linear solver:

```
MM balance after all fills ≥ 0
```

Since markets are largely independent, not all orders fill in any given batch. The solver reasons about the **joint outcome** of all fills and determines which combination is feasible.

### The Result

| | Normal User (\$100) | Flash Quote MM (\$100) |
|--|---|---|
| Orders per market | \$100 bid + \$100 ask | \$100 bid + \$100 ask |
| Markets quoted | 1 | **10+** |
| Total order exposure | \$200 | **\$2,000** |
| Capital efficiency | 2x | **~20x** |

<Info>
This is only possible in a batch auction. All orders resolve simultaneously, so the solver can reason about the joint outcome. On a CLOB, orders execute sequentially — you can't guarantee which combination fills, so you must lock collateral per order.
</Info>

## How It Works

### Solver Constraint

The matching engine solves a welfare-maximization problem. For Flash Quote MMs, it adds the constraint that the MM's net cash flow across all filled orders must not exceed their balance:

```
Σ (cost of filled buy orders) - Σ (proceeds from filled sell orders) ≤ balance
```

The solver activates the combination of MM orders that **maximizes total welfare** while respecting this constraint.

### Order Selection

When an MM's potential fills exceed their budget, the solver prioritizes by **welfare per unit capital**:

| Order | Welfare | Capital Used | Ratio | Status |
|-------|---------|--------------|-------|--------|
| Order A | \$50 | \$200 | 0.25 | Activated |
| Order B | \$30 | \$150 | 0.20 | Activated |
| Order C | \$20 | \$300 | 0.07 | **Not activated** |

Orders creating more value per dollar of MM capital get priority.

<Warning>
Your Flash Quote orders may not all fill even if individually they would. The solver optimizes globally within your balance constraint.
</Warning>

## Slot Auctions

Flash Quoting slots are **scarce** — limited to ensure computational tractability.

### Why Limited Slots?

The matching engine solves an optimization problem. More MMs = more constraints = slower solving. Limiting slots keeps batch processing fast.

### Auction Mechanism

<Steps>
  <Step title="Bidding Period">
    MMs submit sealed bids for slots. Bids are private until auction closes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Winner Selection">
    Top N bidders win slots (e.g., N = 10).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Uniform Pricing">
    All winners pay the (N+1)th highest bid. This incentivizes truthful bidding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Slot Activation">
    Winners can use Flash Quoting for the period (e.g., 1 month).
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Example

10 slots available. Bids received:

| Bidder | Bid |
|--------|-----|
| MM-1 | \$10,000 |
| MM-2 | \$8,000 |
| MM-3 | \$7,500 |
| ... | ... |
| MM-10 | \$5,000 |
| MM-11 | \$4,500 |

**Winners**: MM-1 through MM-10
**Price paid**: \$4,500 each (11th bid)

### Why Second-Price?

- **Truthful bidding**: You can't benefit from underbidding
- **No buyer's remorse**: You never overpay vs the marginal winner
- **Efficient allocation**: Slots go to those who value them most

## Comparison

| Aspect | Normal User | Flash Quote MM |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Collateral | Locked per order | **Solver-enforced balance constraint** |
| Capital efficiency | 2x | **~20x** (scales with markets) |
| Order exposure | ≤ balance | **Far exceeds balance** |
| Order selection | All fill if matched | Solver picks welfare-optimal subset |
| Access | Open | **Auctioned slots** |
| Cost | 5 bps fill fee | Slot auction + 5 bps fill fee |

## Fee Structure

Flash Quote MMs pay:

1. **Slot auction price** — Monthly, uniform second-price
2. **Fill fees** — Same as regular users (currently 5 bps)

The slot auction is the price of ~20x capital efficiency. Fill fees are standard.
