# Core Concepts

## Markets

Sybil markets are **binary prediction markets**. Each market has two outcomes: **YES** and **NO**.

<Info>
YES + NO always equals \$1.00. If you own 1 YES share and 1 NO share, you can merge them into \$1.00.
</Info>

### Example

**Market**: "Will Bitcoin exceed \$100k by Dec 31, 2025?"

| Outcome | Price | Implied Probability |
|---------|-------|---------------------|
| YES | \$0.65 | 65% |
| NO | \$0.35 | 35% |

If Bitcoin exceeds \$100k, YES shares pay \$1.00 each. NO shares pay \$0.00.

### Multi-Outcome Events

Events with more than two outcomes (e.g., "Who wins the election?") are represented as **multiple binary markets**:

- "Candidate A wins?" YES/NO
- "Candidate B wins?" YES/NO
- "Candidate C wins?" YES/NO

The matching engine ensures prices stay consistent across related markets.

## Orders

### Limit Orders

The primary order type. Specifies the maximum price you'll pay (for buys) or minimum price you'll accept (for sells).

```
Buy 100 YES shares at max $0.55
```

This order will fill at \$0.55 or better (lower).

### Multi-Market Orders

Every order is a [payoff vector](/trading/order-types#the-payoff-vector). Multi-market orders specify payoffs across combinations of outcomes, enabling conditional bets, hedges, and spreads. Execute atomically — all or nothing.

```
Payoff vector: [0, 0, 0, 1]  →  "A wins AND B wins"
```

The engine uses boolean algebra on payoff vectors to detect and resolve arbitrage automatically.

### All-or-Nothing (AON)

Orders that must fill completely or not at all. No partial fills.

```
Buy 1000 YES shares AON at max $0.55
```

### Conditional Orders

Orders that only activate if another market's price meets a condition.

```
Buy YES on Market B if Market A price > $0.70
```

## Batch Auctions

Instead of continuous trading, Sybil collects orders over a **batch interval** (e.g., 12 seconds) and processes them simultaneously.

### Clearing Price

The single price at which all trades in a batch execute. Determined by the matching engine to maximize welfare.

| Order | Side | Limit | Fills? |
|-------|------|-------|--------|
| Alice | Buy | 70% | Yes — pays 60% |
| Bob | Buy | 65% | Yes — pays 60% |
| Carol | Sell | 50% | Yes — receives 60% |
| Dave | Sell | 55% | Yes — receives 60% |
| Eve | Buy | 58% | No — below clearing |
| Frank | Sell | 62% | No — above clearing |

**Clearing price: 60%.** All buyers with limits ≥ 60% and all sellers with limits ≤ 60% get filled — at exactly 60%.

### Why Batch Auctions?

| Continuous Trading | Batch Auctions |
|--------------------|----------------|
| Speed wins (bots advantage) | Everyone equal |
| Front-running possible | No front-running |
| Price manipulation easier | Fair price discovery |
| Wider spreads (defensive) | Tighter spreads |

## Flash Quoting (Market Makers)

Flash Quoting lets market makers submit orders **far exceeding their balance** — typically ~20x. The matching engine's solver ensures the MM's balance never goes negative, making this safe without per-order collateral locking. Only possible in a batch auction.

Access is via **auctioned slots** (monthly, sealed-bid, second-price). See [Flash Quoting](/trading/flash-quoting) for details.

## Welfare

**Welfare** = total surplus created by trades.

| Trader | Side | Limit | Clearing | Welfare |
|--------|------|-------|----------|---------|
| Alice | Buy | \$0.60 | \$0.55 | +\$0.05/share |
| Bob | Sell | \$0.50 | \$0.55 | +\$0.05/share |

Alice was willing to pay \$0.60 but only paid \$0.55. Bob was willing to sell at \$0.50 but received \$0.55. Both captured surplus. Sybil's matching engine finds the set of fills that **maximizes total welfare** — mathematically optimal. See [Matching Engine](/technical/matching-engine) for the full pipeline.

## Collateral (sUSDS)

All positions are collateralized in **sUSDS** — Sky's yield-bearing stablecoin.

### Benefits

- **Yield while locked**: Your collateral earns ~4% APY
- **Single token**: Simplifies accounting and risk
- **No rebasing**: Balance changes via rate, not share count


## ZK Proofs

Every batch generates a **zero-knowledge proof** that verifies:

<Check>Matching was computed correctly</Check>
<Check>All trades satisfy limit price constraints</Check>
<Check>Balances are conserved (no money created/destroyed)</Check>
<Check>State transition is valid</Check>

The proof reveals **nothing** about individual trades. Anyone can verify on-chain. See [ZK Proofs](/technical/zk-proofs) for the technology.

## Validium

Sybil is a **validium** — a Layer 2 that:

- Keeps data **off-chain** (cheaper, more private)
- Posts **proofs on-chain** (security, verifiability)

### Why Validium?

| Approach | Data | Proofs | Cost | Privacy |
|----------|------|--------|------|---------|
| L1 | On-chain | N/A | $$$ | None |
| Rollup | On-chain | On-chain | $$ | Low |
| **Validium** | Off-chain | On-chain | $ | **High** |

Posting 10k individual orders to Ethereum would be prohibitively expensive. Posting a single ZK proof covering 10k orders costs almost nothing.

## Escape Hatch

If the sequencer goes offline, you can still withdraw. Your encrypted balance is stored on L1 as events — decrypt it with your P256 key, generate a ZK proof, submit to the L1 contract. Funds released immediately — no challenge period, no off-chain data needed. See [Validium](/technical/validium#escape-hatch) for details.
